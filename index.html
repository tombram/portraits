Biography


My first career was spent in advertising and design in Montreal and Toronto, and culminated with my own product design and consumer research consultancy in Toronto in the 80’s and 90’s.


In portraiture I’m primarily self-taught. I also attended the workshops of Ray Kinstler at the Art Students League in New York, and with Charles Sovek in Massachusetts. I have taught a very limited number of workshops in Knowlton, Quebec, including a course in portraiture at Bishops University (Knowlton Campus) Quebec.



Artist Statement


"Wherever originality comes to the fore there is a chance for beauty. One can love the look of Hals or Raeburn, and at the same time admire the portraits of Schiele and van Gogh. But originality remains the shining coin in the sand that turns our head and fills us with hope."


I accept Portrait Commissions and, on occasion teach premier coup portraiture, classical portraiture and sculpting the head in clay.

Sculpting the Head


Portrait painting is just like sculpting but in 2D. Yet the dominant dimension of any head is its depth – front to back. It’s the dimension easiest for the painter to lose in his depictions...


The nose bridge to the corner of the eye, the inside corner to the outside corner of the eye, the muzzle to the earhole, the upper lip in profile to the lower lip, and on . . .


Nothing beats a quick, even rough head study in clay. It never fails to impress me with how dramatic these receding differences actually are in all of us. In the same vein I stress the premier coup or alla prima approach to head studies.
